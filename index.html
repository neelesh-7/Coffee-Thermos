Coffee Thermos

A coffee thermos uses vacuum technology to keep warm drinks at their temperature for many hours. There are different kinds of thermoses; ones that keep drinks cold and ones that keep drinks warm. I will focus on the thermoses that keep drinks warm. It is important to know that a coffee thermos can only slow down the rate at which the heat escapes, not completely stop it, and eventually the drink will reach room temperature. The coffee thermos has a very minimal amount of heat escaping, this is a constant rate.

What are They?

Coffee thermoses come in different shapes, sizes and prices. They are very effective with their Vacuum Insulation Technology (VIT), which has been in use for over 100 years. Coffee thermoses have different ways of using them. Some have a sliding opening on the lid or unique designs like a leak-proof design for safe transportation. However, the feature that stands above all the rest is ease of use. The best coffee thermoses easily open, close and pour, they don’t spill and are lightweight. Companies sell a variety of coffee thermoses in the world. Each coffee thermos has a special feature that helps them stand out from the rest. Like the Stanley Classic Legendary Bottle, which is extremely durable and can keep your drink warm for quite a long time.

History of Coffee Thermoses

In 1892, James Dewar, a Scottish scientist working at Cambridge University, needed something that could help keep his cold liquid oxygen at -300°F (-184.44°C). He came up with a thermos-like invention that was able to keep the liquid oxygen at its temperature without absorbing the heat from its surroundings. Then in 1904, Thermos LLC thought of incorporating that idea into these insulated food and beverage containers. Many years later, Taiyo Nippon Sanso Corporation sold the world’s first vacuum thermos in 1978. Since then they have improved the quality and the variety of thermoses. The term “thermos” comes from the company, Thermos LLC, which initially had the idea of incorporating it into the food and drinks industry. From 1910 to 1922, it was practically free advertising for them. In 1923, the name finally got registered, making it official.

How do Coffee Thermoses Work?

A coffee thermos uses a certain type of thermal insulation to reduce the transfer of heat as much as possible from the system (warm drink) to the surroundings (air outside the thermos). A coffee thermos has an inner wall and an outer wall that has a low-pressure area in between them acting as thermal insulation. This is a vacuum seal which is an airless void, one that doesn’t have any air or matter between the two walls. This vacuum is a great way to prevent heat transfer by eliminating conduction and convection, as learned in grade 11, to try and keep the temperature inside as warm as possible. The inner wall of the vacuum seal is made of either stainless steel or glass walls which can retain the heat. The inner wall is covered with a reflective silver layer that reduces the amount of heat that is lost by bouncing the heat radiation back into the beverage. Then to cover up the drink, there is an insulated lid to keep heat from escaping at the top and from accidental spills or leaks.

Let’s get more into this vacuum seal which is called the Vacuum Insulation Technology (VIT). It is a double or multi-layered system creating an extremely low-pressure area in between. There is a Vacuum Insulation Panel (VIP) that covers the vacuum making it air-tight. This panel is made from fumed silicon which is from silicon dioxide, and is one of the lightest substances that exist. This panel insulates about 10 times better than any other insulation materials by being able to eliminate gas conduction because of the vacuum, using a material that isn’t flammable, and sealing the material with a gas-vapor-tight film. This method is effective and is guaranteed to last a lifetime.

Usability

Using a coffee thermos is pretty simple. It is wise to warm up the thermos before pouring your drink using hot water. Letting it sit there for a couple of minutes, and then emptying it helps your beverage to be warmer for a little longer. After you pour the warm drink into the thermos, make sure not to overfill it, as it would make it easy to leak (you don’t want that while driving). Once you have poured the warm drink, you can put the lid on tightly. Creating a seal that prevents the heat from escaping. Once you have drunk the beverage, make sure to clean the thermos properly to prevent any stains or bad odor. Before throwing it in the dishwasher, make sure to check if your coffee thermos is dishwasher safe.

Relation to Thermochemistry

The way a coffee thermos functions is similar to a coffee cup calorimeter because they both use insulating materials that focus on minimizing heat transfer, both in and out. They both have an insulated lid at the top, with 2 insulated walls. Undergoing both an endothermic and exothermic reaction depending on the system. If the system (a drink in a thermos and a solution in a calorimeter) is warmer than the surroundings (air for a thermos and H2O for a calorimeter) then it will be an exothermic reaction. A thermos also changes temperature (ΔT = Tf - Ti), and the lowest it will ever go will be room temperature. Finding the enthalpy (ΔH) for an exothermic reaction is similar to finding the enthalpy for a coffee cup calorimeter. Knowing that ΔHrxn would be negative because the energy released is greater than the energy absorbed. Thus energy was lost. You would go about solving for ΔH using the following equations: Qsurr = mcΔT (m is the mass of the warm liquid in the thermos, c is the specific heat of the air, ΔT is the change in temperature that the warm liquid experienced), then Qrxn = -Qsurr, and finally ΔHrxn = -Qrxn/n (n is the number of moles used in the reaction - Limiting Reactant).

Let’s say that I pour hot chocolate at 80°C into a 500mL coffee thermos with the room temperature at 22°C. Let’s assume that the specific heat for hot chocolate is similar to water’s — 4.184J/g°C. We can also assume that 1g is equal to 1mL, so the mass of the hot chocolate is 500g. We can substitute it in the equation which looks like this: Qsurr = (500g)(4.184 J/g°C)(58°C). This equals to 121,336J, which is 121.336kJ. Now we can find the moles, n and then solve for ΔHrxn, which would be a negative number, using the -Qrxn/n equation (Qrxn = -Qsurr).

Application/Impacts

The Vacuum Insulation Panels used by coffee thermoses are used in construction, appliances, and storage units. In homes, buildings and other areas that use heating or air conditioning, Vacuum Insulation Panels are placed inside the walls, behind the drywall and in between the wood/metal rods.

Coffee thermoses are used to keep beverages warm for several hours. They are used by people who are always busy and want to enjoy their warm homemade drink on the go. Coffee thermoses are very popular among campers, hikers and other outdoor enthusiasts. They can take warm drinks on thrilling adventures with a warm meal outdoors. Instead of always buying Tim Hortons to work every day, you can make any homemade drink and drink it on the way with your coffee thermos. Using a reusable coffee thermos instead of disposable cups is a much more environmentally friendly option. It helps society take that step forward to create a more sustainable environment.

Unlike a coffee thermos that keeps drinks warm, NASA’s astronaut spacesuits keep the astronaut cool from the intense heat of the sun when in space. You can say that the suit has a similar function to a thermos that keeps food or drinks cold. When astronauts worked long hours in space, their suits weren't able to maintain a constant temperature. So NASA scientists created new spacesuits that have phase-changing materials (PCM). Phase-change materials can absorb, hold and release heat to keep the person or people inside comfortable. It is used in blankets, bed sheets, and other clothing. This has also been implemented in the thermal control of the spacecraft, to maintain the temperature inside. “Phase change material heat exchangers are perfectly suited for maintaining a habitable environment and component temperatures in low lunar orbit. If we didn’t include this kind of system in Orion, the crew would get too hot and then too cold.” (Henson, 2023)

Companies are now developing products that use vacuum sealing technology to meet certain needs in the medical industry. Such products include small insulin containers and donor organ transportation boxes. These products need to maintain the perfect temperature. This shows that coffee thermoses have a bigger impact on the environment and society than you might think.

Having the knowledge of how coffee thermoses work, benefits you now and in the long run. It shows how such a simple engineering method can be used for space travel. To understand that such a simple idea is able to create breakthroughs in many industries other than just one. That is how humans innovate. By building on the old ideas to create new ones. Making a small, affordable project and then implementing it to bigger projects that can save many lives. Science is the future, the present and the past.

Advantages

    Coffee thermoses provide a variety of advantages like:
  1. Keep a warm drink warm for many hours. This helps you enjoy the drink at an enjoyable temperature hours later.
  2. Portable and easy to use, making them the choice of your container for traveling and other outdoor expeditions.
  3. Durable, and can withstand consumer wear and tear.
  4. Affordable, so you can save money on these miscellaneous items and save on the big things. Saves the stress and cost of buying beverages every day when on the go.
  5. Environment friendly because you drink less in disposable cups that you need to throw out, reducing our waste.
  6. Come in a variety of sizes, designs, and colors, with many different options appealing to certain demographics with certain preferences.
  7. Leak-proof designs, making them safe and trustworthy to carry around or be beaten up in a bag.

Disadvantages

    Even though coffee thermoses are reliable, some issues arise:
  1. Retaining the smell of the most commonly used beverage in the thermos if you don’t clean it properly. This disrupts the smell and taste of other beverages you might drink.
  2. Some aren’t designed for ease of use and have complaints about the leakage for every sip.
  3. Some may look good, but sometimes are very easily destroyed with scratches.
  4. Difficulty cleaning because of the limited opening in the lid, or the stains that build over time.
  5. Some are heavy and don’t fit in cup holders. This will give you lots of discomfort when driving, and potentially even make you go back to disposable cups.
  6. Some don’t keep drinks hot for as long as you might expect.
